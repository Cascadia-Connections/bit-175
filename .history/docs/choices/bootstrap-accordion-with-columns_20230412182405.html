Expanding text

Multiple lists, in multiple columns, via Bootstrap

In this example I made two rows of two columns each. This does mean expanding an item in row 1 pushes the whole page down, which might not be what you want.

List one

An accordion is a kind of expanding text, used to show (and hide) HTML content. It often displays as a list of items stacked on top of one another. When clicked on (or triggered by a keyboard interaction or screen reader), these headers will either reveal or hide associated content.

There are all kinds of uses. It's often used for an FAQ (frequently asked questions), a troubleshooting guide, or a glossary.

It's often used to save space, to let people scan just the categories or questions, find the one they want, and then expand it to see the answer. They don't have to scroll through a bunch of content that's not pertinent to what they're trying to do.

List two

Accordions are a family of box-shaped musical instruments of the bellows-driven free reed aerophone type, colloquially referred to as a squeezebox.

The accordion is used in popular, dance-pop, folk, cajun, zydeco, jazz, and klezmer music, and in both solo and orchestral performances of classical music.

Maybe because you're an accordionist?

According to the Internets, the essential characteristic of the accordion is to combine in one instrument a melody section, also called the diskant, usually on the right-hand manual, with an accompaniment or Basso continuo functionality on the left-hand.
